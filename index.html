// About

Policy fluency.
Technical leadership.

I'm a first-generation Yemeni American, raised in California's Central Valley, where my family's struggles navigating institutions as immigrants shaped my belief that government should be more accessible. That conviction led me to public service as a Jesse Unruh Assembly Fellow and has guided my 14 years in the California State Legislature.

From analyzing legislation to spearheading modernization with artificial intelligence, I've built a career at the intersection of policy and technology. In my current role as Principal Technology Consultant in the Assembly Rules Committee, I lead initiatives that strengthen transparency and efficiency across the entire Assembly.

I'm also a candidate for the Harvard Kennedy School American Service Fellowship (MC/MPA) — a fully-funded program investing in public servants committed to making lasting impact in their communities.

Impact in action.

01 — DIGITAL MODERNIZATION

California State Assembly Website Redesign

Led a comprehensive redesign of the Assembly's public-facing website, guiding a team of technologists, policy staff, and accessibility advocates to rebuild around user needs, plain language, and open data — transforming a confusing legacy site into a transparent civic resource.

↳ Millions of Californians now use this site to track legislation & contact representatives
02 — AI & AUTOMATION

Assembly-Wide AI Integration

Spearheaded integration of artificial intelligence tools across the California State Assembly, digitizing paper-driven legacy processes and streamlining operations for hundreds of legislative offices — representing one of the most significant modernization efforts in Assembly history.

↳ Streamlined operations across hundreds of offices statewide
03 — TRANSPARENCY LEGISLATION

Higher Education Board Meeting Transparency

Helped advance legislation requiring California's public universities and community colleges to record and post their board meetings online — opening critical decision-making around tuition, faculty pay, and program closures to public view for the first time.

↳ Empowered students, faculty, and families to hold decision-makers accountable
04 — CIVIC ENGAGEMENT

League of Minority Voters — CA Launch

Co-organized the launch of the inaugural California chapter of the League of Minority Voters, including panels, advocacy campaigns, and a formal strategic partnership with the California Secretary of State to advance statewide minority representation in democracy.
